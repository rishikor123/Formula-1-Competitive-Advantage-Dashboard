# Formula 1 Competitive Advantage Dashboard

An interactive Tableau dashboard that turns more than sixty years of Formula 1 history, from 1950 to 2017, into a clear visual story of how competitive advantage is built, sustained, and lost across constructors, drivers, circuits, and eras.

**Course:** MGMT 479, Data Visualization · Purdue University, Mitchell E. Daniels, Jr. School of Business
**Author:** Rishita Korapati · **Tool:** Tableau · **Data:** Formula 1 Race Data (1950 to 2017), Kaggle

## What it shows
Formula 1 success comes from far more than raw speed. It reflects constructor engineering, race strategy, pit execution, circuit fit, and the ability to adapt over time. Decades of results exist, yet that history usually sits scattered across seasons and tracks rather than combined into one strategic view. This dashboard brings it together for an audience of constructor leadership and performance strategy teams.

Eight coordinated views move from long run dominance down to race level execution and global context:

1. Constructor Wins Over Time, showing the eras when specific teams controlled the grid.
2. Constructor Rank by Season, capturing positional consistency rather than one off success.
3. Driver Wins Within Each Constructor, separating individual talent from the strength of the team system.
4. Wins versus Podiums by Season, a read on how well a team converts strong results into victories.
5. Starting Position versus Final Finish, showing the link between qualifying and result along with race day variability.
6. Constructor Wins by Circuit, a heatmap of where each team specializes.
7. Finishing Position Distribution by Constructor, contrasting steady teams with volatile ones.
8. Global Circuit Distribution and Race Frequency, mapping the global spread of the sport.

## Key takeaways
Long run success rests on the alignment of consistent rankings, efficient race conversion, and adaptability across circuits rather than any single factor. Teams that pair high win counts with stable top rankings hold the strongest position. The gap between podiums and wins points to conversion as a decisive skill, and outcomes relative to starting position show how much in race execution matters beyond qualifying.
